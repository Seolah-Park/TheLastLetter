Legally Valid Forms of Wills

If your will does not have legal validity, the contents you have written may not be reflected when organizing what you leave behind. Therefore, if you have a clear idea of how you want your belongings to be handled, it is important to follow the forms and conditions recognized by Korean law.

1. A Handwritten Will

This form can be written without the help of legal experts.
All contents of a will must be handwritten by you.
At the bottom of the paper, the year, month, and date when the will was written must be noted.
The address of the place where the will was written must also be included at the bottom of the paper.
All detailed information of the address like building numbers, etc., must be fully written.
You must write your full name and affix your seal. A fingerprint seal is acceptable.

2. A Recorded Will

This form is particularly useful when you are unable to write.
However, at least one witness is required to observe the recording process and ensure the accuracy of the will.
During the video or audio recording of the will, all contents must be recorded in your own voice.
When recording begins, you must state the purpose, full name, and the year, month, date without omission.
After you have spoken all the contents, the witness must state their name, and verify that the will has been accurately recorded.
Minors, those who benefit from the will, and those who lack the capacity to handle affairs due to conditions such as dementia cannot serve as witnesses.

3. A Notarial Will

This form is not commonly used but has the most certain legal validity.
Though it requires going through a rigorous process with a notary having legal knowledge,
it can play an important role in situations of conflicting interests.
Two witnesses are required, and they must be capable adults who can handle affairs,
just like the witnesses for a recorded will.
In the presence of two witnesses and the notary, you must state the purpose of the will,
and the notary will write it down and read it back to ensure accuracy.
You and the witnesses must then write your names accurately and affix seals at the bottom of the paper.

4. A Secret Will

This form can be used if you need to keep the contents of the will confidential until your death.
You must write the purpose of the will and your full name.
Unlike a handwritten will, this can be written by another.
This paper must then be placed in an envelope, which must be sealed
so that it cannot be opened without breaking the seal.
Like a recorded will, the envelope must be shown in front of two witnesses who are capable adults.
The year, month, and date must be written on the envelope.
You and the witnesses must write names accurately and affix your seals on the envelope.
The envelope must be confirmed by a notary or court clerk within five days after the date is written on it.

5. An Oral Will

This form is only recognized if you are in a sudden critical condition and cannot prepare a will in other forms.
You must state your will in front of two witnesses who are capable adults, just like for a recorded will.
One of the witnesses must then write down what you have said and read it back to confirm its accuracy.
All witnesses must write their names accurately and affix their seals at the bottom of the paper.
